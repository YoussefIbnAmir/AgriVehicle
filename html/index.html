About us

Group 325

The purpose of Agri-vehicle project was to design a car equipped with various sensors, including UV, temperature, humidity, and moisture sensors, to collect data on farm land in order to better understand and address the issue of desertification in Egypt. The data collected is represented in form of graphs that show the stimulated situation for the specific, for instance putting vinegar in soil stimulating the pH sensor.

The project began with an extensive literature review to gain an understanding of the current state of desertification in Egypt and the specific challenges faced by farmers and agricultural communities. Through this research, the team identified that one of the major issues was the inappropriate growth of plants due to intense UV radiation and the pH of soil not being neutral, leading to phosphorus fixation and other problems that hindered plant growth and many other issues that was caused by data measured by different sensors.

Using the data collected by the vehicle's sensors, the team generated detailed graphs of the affected areas and developed recommendations for farmers and other stakeholders on how to address the identified problems. These recommendations included strategies for improving soil health, reducing exposure to UV radiation, and increasing the effectiveness of irrigation systems. Overall, the project provided valuable insights into the causes of desertification in Egypt and helped to inform the development of effective solutions. The team's work has the potential to make a significant impact on the lives of farmers and communities affected by desertification in Egypt.
